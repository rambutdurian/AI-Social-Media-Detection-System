Use datasets in order of priority. Start with #1–2 for your hackathon prototype, then add #3–4 to strengthen your report.

1. FaceForensics++ (FF++)
Start here
1,000 original videos + manipulations via Deepfakes, Face2Face, FaceSwap, NeuralTextures. The gold-standard benchmark used in virtually every detection paper.
  • ~977 YouTube source videos with frontal faces
  • 3 quality levels: raw, HQ (c23), LQ (c40)
  • Use c23 quality for hackathon — best balance of realism vs file size
  • Requires filling a Google Form to access (academic use)
github.com/ondyari/FaceForensics
2. Celeb-DF v2
High quality fakes
590 real YouTube videos + 5,639 high-quality deepfake videos of celebrities. Harder to detect than FF++ — tests if your model generalises.
  • Diverse ages, ethnicities, and genders
  • Significantly more realistic than early datasets
  • Widely used for cross-dataset evaluation
github.com/yuezunli/celeb-deepfakeforensics
3. DFDC — Deepfake Detection Challenge
Social media focus
Facebook/Meta dataset with 128,154 videos — the largest public deepfake dataset and the most diverse. Specifically designed to reflect social media video conditions.
  • Multiple synthesis methods, varied lighting and angles
  • Available on Kaggle (no form required)
  • Cite: Dolhansky et al. (2020), arXiv:2006.07397
kaggle.com — Deepfake Detection Challenge
4. DeepFake-Eval-2024
Most realistic (2024)
Real-world data sourced directly from social media in 2024. Includes 44 hrs of video, 56.5 hrs of audio, and 1,975 images across 88 websites in 52 languages.
  • Tests how your model handles modern diffusion-based fakes
  • Warning: state-of-the-art models see up to 50% AUC drop on this vs legacy datasets
  • Use it to honestly report real-world performance
github.com/nuriachandra/Deepfake-Eval-2024
5. SDFVD (small-scale, beginner-friendly)
Quick prototyping
Only 106 videos (53 real, 53 fake). Pre-processed to 720p, 4–5 sec clips. Ideal for rapid testing before scaling up to larger datasets.
  • No GPU required to run full evaluation
  • Available on Mendeley Data (open access)
  • Good for verifying your pipeline works end-to-end first
data.mendeley.com — SDFVD
6. HuggingFace — UniDataPro deepfake videos
Easy download
10,000+ files, 7,000+ people. Face-swapped videos with AI overlays sourced from platforms like MagicHour and FaceSwapVideo. Useful for social media context.
huggingface.co/datasets/UniDataPro/deepfake-videos-dataset

Run evaluation on your test split
Always evaluate on a held-out test set (20% of data). Never evaluate on training data — judges will ask about this. Use an 80/10/10 train/val/test split.

These are the 4 metrics you must report in both your technical report and slides. All are standard in published deepfake detection research.

Important: context for your judges
State-of-the-art models on DeepFake-Eval-2024 reach ~78% accuracy. If your cross-dataset AUC drops, that is expected and normal — be upfront about it. Judges respect honesty over inflated numbers.
AUC-ROC
Area under the ROC curve. Measures how well the model separates real from fake across all thresholds. Most important metric in papers.
Target: above 0.85 on FF++
Accuracy
% of videos correctly classified. Easy to understand for non-technical judges. Report separately for real and fake classes.
Target: above 90% on FF++
F1 Score
Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Important when your test set is imbalanced (more fakes than real or vice versa).
Target: above 0.85
Response time (app)
Time from video upload to result displayed in the browser. This is a UX metric — directly tied to 20 usability points.
Target: under 5 seconds
